WHAT IS CACTUS : A cactus is a type of plant belonging to the family Cactaceae, known for its ability to thrive in arid and desert environments. Cacti are well-adapted to conserve water, typically having thick, fleshy stems that store moisture. Their spines, which are modified leaves, help reduce water loss and offer protection from herbivores.
Key characteristics of cacti include:
  • Succulent stems that store water.
  • Spines in place of leaves, reducing evaporation.
  • Ability to survive in harsh, dry climates.
  • Many cacti produce beautiful flowers, which can range from small to large, in various colors.
